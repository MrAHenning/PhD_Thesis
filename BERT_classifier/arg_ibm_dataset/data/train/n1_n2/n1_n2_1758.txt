To violate intellectual property is no different morally than violating other property rights The term intellectual property can be found used in an October 1845 Massachusetts Circuit Court ruling in the patent case Davoll et al. v. Brown., in which Justice Charles L. Woodbury wrote that "only in this way can we protect intellectual property, the labors of the mind, productions and interests are as much a man's own..as the wheat he cultivates, or the flocks he rears."[REF
