Life in the south under rocket barrages had become unbearable In May 2007, a significant increase in rocket attacks from Gaza prompted the temporary evacuation of thousands of residents from Sderot [REF]. According to the United Nations, 40 percent of the city's residents left in the last two weeks of May [REF]. During the summer of 2007, 3,000 of the city's 22,000 residents (comprising mostly the city's key upper and middle class residents) left for other areas, out of rocket range
