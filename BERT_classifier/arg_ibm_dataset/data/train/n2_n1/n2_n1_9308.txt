Social disorganization theory is based on the work of Henry McKay and Clifford R. Shaw of the Chicago School [REF]. Social disorganization theory postulates that neighborhoods plagued with poverty and economic deprivation tend to experience high rates of population turnover [REF]. These neighborhoods also tend to have high population heterogeneity [REF]. With high turnover, informal social structure often fails to develop, which in turn makes it difficult to maintain social order in a community crime rates are associated with poverty
