Social control theory, which is among the most popular theories in criminology,[REF] proposes that crime is most commonly perpetrated by individuals who lack strong bonds or connections with their social environment [REF]. Based upon Travis Hirschi's Causes of Delinquency (1969), social bonding theory pioneered the notion that criminologists can gain useful insight into the motives behind criminal behavior by examining what normally motivates individuals to refrain from crime. From this it is argued that, in those segments of the population where such motivation is lacking, crime will be more prevalent.
