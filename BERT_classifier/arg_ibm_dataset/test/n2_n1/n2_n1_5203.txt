Reproductive rights began to develop as a subset of human rights at the United Nation's 1968 International Conference on Human Rights [REF]. The resulting non binding Proclamation of Teheran was the first international document to recognize one of these rights when it stated that: "Parents have a basic human right to determine freely and responsibly the number and the spacing of their children."[REF][REF Parents have the exclusive right to determine freely and responsibly the number and spacing of their children
