Aid to underdeveloped countries has sometimes been criticized as being more in the interest of the donor than the recipient A notable example of this is US aid to fund HIV/AIDS treatment in Africa. According to Thalif Deen's [REF] Tied Aid Strangling Nations,Washington conditioned African governments to purchase anti- HIV/AIDS drugs only from American companies which costs $15,000 annually to a patient who lives below a $1.00 a day compared to purchasing South African made generic drugs that would cost $350 annually. This ultimately makes the poor country worse off
