Media effects theories in modern times originated with Bandura's social learning theory, which suggests that children may learn aggression from viewing others [REF]. Modeling of behavior was observed in Bandura's Bobo Doll experiments. Bandura showed children a video of a model beating up a Bobo doll and then put the children in a room with a Bobo doll to see if he/she would imitate the behavior previously seen on the video.   The findings of this experiment suggest that children tended to model the behavior they witnessed in the video children may imitate aggressive behaviors witnessed in media
